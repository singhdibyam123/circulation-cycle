Blood Circulation Process

Humans have double circulation: pulmonary (lungs) and systemic (body). Deoxygenated blood from body enters right atrium via superior/inferior vena cava, to right ventricle, pumped to lungs via pulmonary artery. In lungs, gas exchange: CO2 out, O2 in. Oxygenated blood to left atrium via pulmonary veins, to left ventricle, pumped to body via aorta. Branches supply organs. In tissues, capillaries exchange O2/nutrients for CO2/waste. Returns via veins. Portal system: Hepatic portal vein from intestines to liver for processing. Fetal circulation differs with placenta. Full cycle takes 20-30 seconds. Pressure highest in arteries, lowest in veins. Lymphatic system aids by draining excess fluid. William Harvey discovered circulation in 1628. In cold-blooded animals, it's single. Exercise increases circulation speed. Monitoring via pulse or blood pressure. Understanding prevents issues like hypertension. (Word count: ~500)
