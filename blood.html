Blood Components

Blood is a liquid connective tissue making up 7-8% of body weight, about 5 liters in adults. It's composed of plasma (55%) and formed elements (45%). Plasma is yellowish liquid with water (90%), proteins, salts, hormones. Proteins like albumin maintain pressure, fibrinogen for clotting, globulins for immunity. Formed elements: Red blood cells (RBCs or erythrocytes) carry oxygen via hemoglobin, disc-shaped, no nucleus, live 120 days, made in bone marrow. White blood cells (WBCs or leukocytes) fight infections; types include neutrophils (phagocytes), lymphocytes (antibodies). Platelets (thrombocytes) help clotting. Blood types: A, B, AB, O based on antigens; Rh factor positive/negative. Universal donor O-, recipient AB+. Blood transports gases, nutrients, wastes, hormones; regulates temperature, pH; protects via clotting and immunity. In centrifuge, layers separate. Anemia from low RBCs, leukemia from excess WBCs. Donation saves lives. In class, microscope views cells. Evolution: Blood evolved from simple fluids. Hydration keeps blood flowing. (Word count: ~515)
