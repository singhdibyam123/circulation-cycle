Functions of the Circulatory System

The primary function is transport: Delivering oxygen from lungs to cells, nutrients from digestion to tissues, hormones from glands to targets, waste to kidneys/lungs/liver. It regulates body temperature by redistributing heat – skin vessels dilate to cool, constrict to warm. Maintains pH balance by buffering acids/bases. Protects via immunity (WBCs fight pathogens) and clotting (prevents blood loss). Supports homeostasis. In pregnancy, increases volume for fetus. During fight-or-flight, adrenaline boosts heart rate. Nutrition: Iron for hemoglobin, vitamins for vessels. Dysfunction affects all systems. In space, microgravity causes fluid shifts. Historical: Bloodletting was misguided treatment. Modern: Transplants save lives. Education promotes heart-healthy habits. (Word count: ~510)
